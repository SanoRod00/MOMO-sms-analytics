# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We — the members of **Underrated Silicon Valley** and all contributors to the MOMO SMS Analytics project — pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, background, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and productive community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behaviour

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologise to those affected, and learn from the experience.
- Focus on what is best for the project and the team, not just individual preferences.
- Credit teammates' contributions fairly in commits, PRs, and documentation.

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information (real name, contact details, location) without explicit permission.
- Deliberately claiming credit for work done by others.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional academic setting.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — the GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, code reviews, commit messages), the team WhatsApp group, Jira board comments, and any other space where team members represent this project.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Incidents can be reported to the current **Scrum Master** (see [docs/AGILE.md](./docs/AGILE.md)) or directly to any team lead via private message. All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion.

### Response process

| Severity | Description | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Correction** | Inappropriate language or minor unprofessional behaviour | Private written warning; clarification of expected behaviour |
| **2 — Warning** | A single serious incident or repeated minor incidents | Formal warning; temporary removal from team communication channels may apply |
| **3 — Removal** | Severe or sustained violations | Removal from the project and escalation to ALU academic staff if warranted |

All enforcement decisions are final once agreed upon by the majority of the core team.
